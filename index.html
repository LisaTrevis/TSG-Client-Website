About Me

I've always been happiest with my fingers in the dirt, and I tend each garden as if it were my own. Finding and reusing materials is a secret passion of mine, and free and upcycled items seem to find a way into my gardens.

There She Grows, CA was started in collaboration with my Graduate study in Soil and Water Science. I focus on growing food in urban environments while creating habitats for pollinators and beneficial insects. I'm currently working with a bee mentor and plan to offer backyard beekeeping as a service in the future.

About There She Grows CA

There She Grows CA is a fine gardening company specializing in the transformation of residential properties away from water-hungry grass lawns to edible and natural drought-resistent landscapes.

I sold my first landscaping company in 2012, and realized I wanted my next venture to focus on restorative practices and giving back to the community.

I offer initial design, cleanups, maintenance, aquaponics set-ups, and long-term consultations. My main goal, however, is to provide my clients with a low maintenance landscape that can regenerate itself year after year.

Gardening Services

  • Free initial consultation. Your design can be an overall transformation, small projects around the yard, or a combo.
  • Crowd out lawn weeds with natural methods and avoid chemicals in your landscaping and gardens with hand weeding.
  • As nutrients are absorbed by plants and diluted by rain, they need to be replaced. Chemical fertilizers only provide a few nutrients. For truly healthy plants, full spectrum organic fertilization is the best option.
  • Mulch is an easy way to create beautiful curb appeal and habitat for beneficial insects and microbes. It also helps plants retain moisture.

Specialties

Raised Herb & Vegetable Gardens

Imagine fresh seasonal produce available just outside your back door that is organically grown (no toxic chemicals), local (reduces your impact on the environment), and sustainable (uses natural methods to build soil, retain water, and grow healthy plants).

Bee & Butterfly Habitats

Bees and butterflies are not only beautiful they are beneficial too. These pollinators hold the key to our food supply.  Increasing certain flowering plants helps them survive through the tough, dry summer season in California.

Eco-Friendly Mulch

Mulch not only improves the appearance of your landscape but also serves as a ground cover that protects the soil, conserves water, and provides homes for beneficial organisms like insects, worms, and fungi. These all contribute to the health of your garden.

Drought-Tolerant & Native Plants

California native plants are beautiful and provide food and shelter for native birds, animals, and insects. They also flourish with the natural weather patterns here without additional fertilizers and wasted water.

Frequently Asked Questions

1) What is your service area?

I currently service Alameda, Bay Farm Island, and parts of Oakland.

2) I just want my lawn mowed each week. Do you do that?

My focus is on converting water wasting lawns to edible gardens and natural habitats for beneficial bees and butterflies. However, lawn care can be included in the maintenance schedule.

3) Is there a minimum service commitment?

No. Services are customized to individual client needs and dependent on the season.

4) I'd like to be here when you provide the services. Is that possible?

Absolutely. I coordinate with clients to determine the best day and time for everyones' schedules, which may include evenings and weekends as needed.

5) I live in a condo and only have a small patio space. Do you service small spaces?

Yes. I can make any outdoor space more welcoming with container gardening, clean-up, and trimming. Also, my battery powered equipment is quieter than gas equipment and produces zero gas pollution. Your neighbors will thank you!

6) What is the benefit of edible gardens and pollinator habitats?

With the growing concern over pesticides and the high cost of organic food and herbs, an edible garden is an investment for both your wallet and your family's health. Pollinator habitats are not only beautiful, but also help combat bee Colony Collapse Disorder and encourage butterflies to visit your yard.

7) Why is your focus on edible gardens and bee & butterfly habitats?

Food deserts, where people do not have access to affordable, fresh and healthy food, are an increasing problem. Container and raised bed gardens can be installed anywhere, and families who grow their own food are more likely to eat better and live a healthier lifestyle. Encouraging specific types of insects to live in your garden not only helps to pollinate your plants, but also helps to control the pest populations that can damage and eat your vegetables and herbs.

8) I love to garden but don't know what I'm doing. Can I help?

Absolutely! I'm happy to teach you how to care for your garden and/or landscape. You can contribute as much or as little as you wish.
